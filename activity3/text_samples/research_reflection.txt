Research Reflection: AI Assistance in Academic Work

Working with AI tools improved my speed in drafting and summarizing technical material, but it also revealed how easy it is to accept outputs without enough verification. During this week, I used AI to generate starter code, rewrite documentation, and propose test cases.

What worked well:
- Rapid brainstorming for algorithm options
- Clear first drafts for reports
- Fast generation of edge-case ideas

What did not work well:
- Occasional overconfident explanations
- Missing assumptions in generated code
- Inconsistent handling of ambiguous requirements

My main takeaway is that AI should be treated as a collaborative assistant, not an automatic authority. I now review each output with a checklist: verify facts, test code, and confirm alignment with assignment goals. This process reduced errors and improved confidence in the final submission.
